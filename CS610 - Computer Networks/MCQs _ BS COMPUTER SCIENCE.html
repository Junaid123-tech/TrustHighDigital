Question No: 36 ( M a r k s: 1 )

The product of delay and throughput measures the _____ of data that can be present on the network.

► Area

► Volume (Page 80)

► Length

► None of the given

DELAY:
It is an important quantitative property of networks. Delay is a measure how long
it takes for a bit of data to travel across the network from one compute to the other. It is measured in seconds or fractions of seconds.

THROUGHPUT:
Throughput is a measure of the rate at which data can be sent through the
network. The throughput capability of the underlying hardware is called bandwidth. Because each frame contains headers, the effective throughput is less than the hardware bandwidth. Networking professional often use the term speed as a synonym for throughput.
